Augmented Reality


Augmented reality is when a device creates a computer generated image over reality, changing a user's view of the real world. Although augmented reality has been around for decades, it has recently risen to the attention of the public with its success on the Nintendo 3DS and the Oculus Rift.


  • The Nintendo 3DS uses its dual cameras to create an image that they are then able to overlay with any image they want. They use these images to create fun, interactive games for the user.
  • The Oculus Rift is an immersive new device that uses dual projection to create a 3D image inside a headset. It uses sensors that pick up head movement and changes the image based on head position. It's being called the future if gaming and already has multiple games in development for it.











NFC


Near Field Communication, or NFC for short, is a way for devices like phones or tablets to communicate without physically connecting. Users are able to wave their smartphones or tablets over any NFC capable device and send information without going through multiple steps setting up a connection.


NFC can be used in a variety of useful ways, including


  • Sharing photos, games, and info between users
  • It can be synced with certain cars, so the car automatically adjusts itself to you
  • You can use it as a virtual credit card
  • It works as a ticket to concerts and busses
